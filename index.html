I am studying Human Centered Computing Human-Computer Intearction at the Georgia Institute of Technology, speicializing in Learning Science Technology, with a minor in computational linguistics. I am advised by Jennifer Kim. I received my M.S. degree from the University of Washington, Seattle in Speech-Language Pathology. Prior to joining this PhD program, I worked as a speech therapist for 10 years in private clinics and public schools, beginning my career at the University of Washington Autism Center , supporting both clinical services and early intervention research studies.
My research interests are in building eduational technology that supports learner agency and personalized learning pathways for students with varied cognitive profiles, including learners with disabilities. I focus on participatory and co-design design methods, where I look for ways that AI can build better relationships between teachers, learners, and peers.
