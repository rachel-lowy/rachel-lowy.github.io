I am a PhD student in Human-Centered Computing at Georgia Tech, where I am advised by Jennifer Kim. My research explores how technology can foster meaningful learning and communication between people with different cognitive profiles. I focus on co-designing in partnership with users to ensure that the process of design technology reflects their lived experiences. I am especially excited about bringing co-design into educational spaces, where we can collaboratively build knowledge and instill learners with a sense of agency in a rapidly evolving technology landscape.

Before beginning my PhD, I spent a decade as a speech therapist in private clinics, public schools, and on research intervention teams. Working as part of teams from the University of Washington Autism Center and Institute for Learning & Brain Sciences (I-LABS) fueled my interest in research and working with a wide spectrum of learners. Seeing the impact of well-designed educational and accessibility tools firsthand continues to drive my research today.

Research Interests

My research centers on designing educational technology that empowers learners by promoting agency and enabling personalized learning pathways, particularly for students with diverse cognitive profiles, including those with disabilities.

I use participatory and co-design methodologies to develop tools that align with the priorities of users, ensuring that designs reflect their lived experiences. A key focus of my work is exploring how AI can foster collaboration and strengthen connections between teachers, learners, and peers to create learning environments that foster meaningful growth.
