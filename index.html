I am studying Human-Computer Interaction at the Georgia Institute of Technology, specializing in Learning Science Technology, with a minor in computational linguistics. I am advised by Jennifer Kim.

I received my M.S. degree from the University of Washington, Seattle in Speech-Language Pathology. Prior to joining this PhD program, I worked as a speech therapist for 10 years in private clinics and public schools, beginning my career at the University of Washington Autism Center.

Research Interests

My research focuses on educational technology that supports learner agency and personalized learning for students with varied cognitive profiles, including learners with disabilities. I use participatory and co-design methods, exploring how AI can improve relationships between teachers, learners, and peers.
